Original framework development

A seven-layer ecosystem for human-centered AI in education

Built independently over five years of teaching, research, and practice — each layer is classroom-tested, grounded in validated theory, and original in combination. Together they form a complete architecture for ethical, pedagogically sound AI integration.

Layer 1 — Ethics & policy
The LTTR Framework

A student-facing ethical decision-making framework embedded in course design. Originally TTR (Traceable, Transparent, Responsible); Literacy was added as the foundational element — students cannot act transparently, trace their process, or exercise responsibility without first being AI-literate.

L
Literate
Recognize ethical dimensions before encountering pressure. Know policies, tools, prompting, evaluation, and citation.
T
Transparent
Disclose AI use openly. Make decisions visible rather than implicit.
T
Traceable
Document the learning process — drafts, prompts, reflections, process logs. Slow down decision-making.
R
Responsible
Align AI use with learning purpose. Ask not whether you can use AI, but whether you should.
Layer 1 — Ethics & policy (continued)
The Safe Ethical Zone (SEZ)

A psychophysics-informed concept describing the dynamic space in which students operate ethically. Students are not consistently ethical or unethical actors — they function within a range shaped by pressure, ambiguity, cognitive load, and environmental stimuli.

Generative AI functions as a threshold-destabilizing force — compressing the distance between ethical intention and unethical action. The SEZ is not an excuse for misconduct. It is a diagnostic tool for designing better conditions. Students in the SEZ are often one stimulus away from crossing an ethical boundary.

Layer 2 — AI literacy
Foundational Framework for GenAI Literacy & Deployment

A five-stage framework guiding students and educators from first access to full adoption. Adopted beyond academia — cited as a core framework in the Michigan Small Business Development Center's guide on AI for Small Businesses.

1
Access
Assess equity, tool availability, and AI literacy baseline.
2
Learn
Guiding principles, ethics, policies, citation models, information security.
3
Explore & Apply
Define goal, create prompts, iterate with AI tool.
4
Reflect
Evaluate output against criteria, revisit ethical guidelines.
5
Adopt
Integrate output, report AI collaboration level.
Layers 3–7 — Overview
The complete ecosystem

Beyond ethics and literacy, the ecosystem addresses every dimension of human-centered AI integration — from how students interact with AI, to how educators build agents, to how outputs are evaluated.

Layer 3
Interaction design
Student-Driven and AI-Driven interaction models. Ask–Review–Reflect–Follow-up cycle. Planned-Structured-Supervised framework. Published as WMU institutional resources.
Layer 4
Agent design
PCTII cycle (Prepare–Create–Test–Improve–Integrate). Educator-in-the-Loop (EITL) two-layer governance architecture. Practitioner-built, code-free, classroom-tested.
Layer 5
Output quality
14 storytelling characteristics (10 primary, 4 secondary) for AI-generated instructional content. Structured intake interface constraining AI within pedagogical boundaries.
Layer 6
Evaluation
Human-AI Collaboration Rubric — 210-point instrument measuring quality of human-AI interaction (110 human tasks, 100 AI tasks).
Layer 7
Critical thinking
Strategies for preventing cognitive dependency and protecting independent thinking in AI-integrated learning environments.

Scholarly identity

I am a human-centered framework scholar working at the intersection of human factors, educational psychology, instructional design, and AI ethics. My core through-line: I build protective design systems for people inside institutions not designed with their full humanity in mind.

I do not just describe problems — I build frameworks, tools, and deployed artifacts that solve them. Over five years of independent work, without a research mentor, I have developed a seven-layer framework ecosystem for ethical AI integration in higher education — while completing a PhD and beginning a faculty position.

Research streams

Stream 1 — AI-Integrated Education: Building human-centered frameworks for ethical, pedagogically sound AI integration in higher education. Seven layers of original framework development, three deployed AI agents, and a publication record spanning peer-reviewed journals, conference proceedings, and institutional resources.

Stream 2 — PhD Research in Ergonomics (WMU, ABD): Using machine learning and computer vision to classify hand movements in repetitive motion tasks to capture how fatigue develops. Interdisciplinary, multi-layered, and methodologically sophisticated.
